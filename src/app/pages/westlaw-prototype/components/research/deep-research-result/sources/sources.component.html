  1. Robinson Helicopter Co., Inc. v. Dana Corp. Supreme Court of California • December 23, 2004 • 34 Cal.4th 979
    AI Summary

    Manufacturer brought action against parts supplier for breach of contract, breach of warranty, and intentional and negligent misrepresentation after supplier changed manufacturing process, which resulted in increase failure rate. The Superior Court, after a jury trial, entered a judgement in favor of the manufacturer in the sum of $7,533,924, including $6 million in punitive damages. Supplier appealed. The Court of Appeal affirmed in part and reversed in part. The Supreme Court granted review, superseding the opinion of the Court of Appearl.
